Introduction to the Dataset

Our interactive visualization focuses on weather data, sourced from the Daily Weather in the U.S., 2017 dataset, which contains daily measurements of various weather conditions. We are particularly interested in analyzing several variables: precipitation, snowfall, and average temperature. By exploring the interactions between these variables, we aim to uncover meaningful patterns related to temperature and precipitation conditions across different times of the year.

The visualizations are designed to answer key questions about how weather conditions vary across time and how they interact with temperature fluctuations. We provide users with interactive tools to explore and uncover hidden patterns, using visual encodings and interactive techniques to make the analysis engaging and intuitive.

Rationale for Design Decisions

Our visualization focuses on the precipitation and snow for different states during the different months. For visual encoding and design choices, we chose scatter plots as they provide a clear way to show relationships between two variables. Colors are used to represent key distinctions, such as snowfall versus rainfall in the scatterplot. The size of the data point also helps make the amount of precipitation clearer to the viewers, enabling them to understand our visualization better.

Interaction Techniques

Month Slider (Dynamic Filter)

The chart is connected by a month slider at the top, allowing users to filter the data by month or season. This interaction helps users focus on specific time periods to analyze temperature and precipitation patterns during specific months (e.g., winter storms or summer heatwaves).

Tooltips

By hovering over data points in the chart, users receive detailed information for that specific day, including temperature, precipitation, and snowfall. This helps users dive deeper into specific weather events of interest.
